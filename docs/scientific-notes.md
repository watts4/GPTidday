# Pathogen Dominion — Scientific Mapping Notes

## Design Principles
- The map uses anatomical continuity and compartment links (e.g., mucosal chains, bloodstream, lymphatic network) as spread constraints.
- Colonization resolves from route compatibility, tissue barrier strength, clearance pressure, and immune surveillance.
- Adaptations are high-level evolutionary abstractions with explicit strategic tradeoffs.

## Mechanic-to-Biology Translation
1. **Region barriers and routes**
   - Regions encode barrier intensity, clearance, immune pressure, and pH context.
   - Spread requires adjacency and a biologically plausible route (mucosal, bloodstream, lymphatic, neural, breach).
2. **Tropism and compatibility**
   - Faction preferred tissues and starter routes model baseline tropism.
   - Broad tropism adaptation increases access but imposes efficiency penalties.
3. **Immune opposition**
   - Innate/adaptive abstraction is represented through immune attention and per-tissue immune values.
   - High visible spread and damage increase attention, raising clearance effectiveness.
4. **Persistence vs virulence tradeoff**
   - Damage and inflammation accelerate host viability loss, risking a pyrrhic failure.
   - Reservoir/persistence stats improve survival but often reduce burst growth potential.
5. **Events and intervention pressure**
   - Fever, generalized treatment pressure, and barrier shifts model changing host context and care intervention.

## Intentional Fictionalization
- “Total Host Dominion” victory is a strategy-game fiction and not a clinical endpoint.
- Treatment effects are abstract pressure modifiers, not medical protocols.
- Adaptation cards are thematic abstractions and intentionally omit actionable laboratory detail.
